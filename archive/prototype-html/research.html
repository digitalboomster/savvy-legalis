Under the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 2020, specifically Section 288 1, a company may by ordinary resolution remove a director before the expiration of their period of office, notwithstanding anything in its articles or in any agreement between it and the director.

The procedural requirements are strict to ensure fair hearing:

  • 01. Special Notice: A special notice of 28 days is required for any resolution to remove a director Longe v. FBN Plc (2010) 2.
  • 02. Right to Represent: The director concerned is entitled to be heard on the resolution at the meeting and can submit a written representation 3.
  • 03. Vacating Office: Once the resolution is passed, the director ceases to hold office immediately, though they may claim damages for breach of contract 5.
lightbulb

Note: Section 288(1) of CAMA 2020 clarifies that this power does not authorize the removal of a director of a private company who has held office for life since the commencement of the previous Act.
